Presentation

ARBOREA (ARchaeoBOtanical geoReferenced rEcords of Ancient Italy) is a new digital tool designed to compile, harmonize, and georeference archaeobotanical data from Central Italy. Developed within the NRRP PE5 CHANGES Spoke 8 and aligned with the BRAIN database, ARBOREA addresses key issues of data dispersion and terminological inconsistency. The database integrates information from over 150 sites and 1400 contexts, cataloguing more than 7000 samples and nearly 500 plant taxa. Structured relationally and supported by dedicated thesauri for plant nomenclature, parts, preservation types, and chronology, it enables advanced, multi-factor queries and comparative analyses. ARBOREA enhances data accessibility and fosters interdisciplinary collaboration, offering a robust platform for reconstructing human-environment interactions across time.

Taxa Affinity Explanation

Conceptual Explanation

Each sample is described by attributes at the sample level (e.g., precise_taxon, Family, genus, s_type, s_part, s_foss, qt) and by attributes inherited from the related context (e.g., typology, chronology_iccd). Affinity between two samples is computed by combining matches across these attributes, with weights that increase for rarer values.

  • Hard rule: similarity is zero unless at least one among precise_taxon, genus, or Family matches.
  • Triplet bonus: a match on the triplet (Family, genus, s_type) yields an additional score scaled by rarity.
  • User control: users may reweight attributes or reset to defaults.

Rarity

Rarity is computed as the inverse square root of global frequencies, so frequent values have reduced impact, while rare taxa or combinations contribute more.

Output

Similarity is returned as a percentage on a 0–100 scale. A radar breakdown visualises per-attribute contributions, including the triplet bonus when present.

Symbol Legend:
  • s, s′ — Samples
  • a_j — Attribute index
  • v — Attribute value
  • T — Triplet (Family, genus, s_type)
  • w(a_j, v) — Weight = rarity × importance
  • λ_j — User-defined importance per attribute
  • sim — Similarity

1. Attribute Weights & Rarity

\( w(a_j, v) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{f(a_j, v)}} \cdot \lambda_j \)

2. Weighted Jaccard

Similarity uses weighted unions/intersections and adds the triplet bonus to the numerator.

3. Triplet Bonus

If values match for all attributes in T, a bonus \( \sum 1/\sqrt{f_T} \) is added.

4. Hard Rule

If none among precise_taxon, genus, or Family match, then \( \text{sim}(s,s') = 0 \).
